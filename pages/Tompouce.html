Tompouce

All Recipes

Ingredients:

  • Puff Pastry
  • Milk (Whole milk is ideal, although anything works)
  • Cornstarch
  • Vanilla (Pure vanilla extract or vanilla bean paste)

Steps:

  1. Begin by making the pastry cream. In a large saucepan, beat the

    egg yolks together, then slowly whisk in the milk. Add sugar and

    cornstarch and whisk until smooth.

  2. Heat the pan over medium heat, whisking constantly until the mixture

    is very thick and easily coats the back of a spoon. Remove from the

    heat, then whisk in vanilla paste or vanilla extract.

  3. Transfer the mixture to a medium mixing bowl. Cover with plastic wrap,

    pressing the plastic against the surface of the custard so it doesn't

    form a skin. Cool completely in the refrigerator.

  4. Preheat the oven to 400°F/200°C. Place the puff pastry sheet on a parchment

    paper lined baking sheet and prick the top with the tines of a fork.

    Bake for about 20 to 24 minutes or until golden brown and crisp.

  5. If it puffs up too much at the start of baking, use a fork to deflate it,

    then lightly press it back down to flatten. Set aside to cool.

  6. Cut the baked puff pastry sheet in half using a serrated knife. It should

    be roughly 9 by 6 inches after halving (the exact dimensions will vary by

    puff pastry brand!).

  7. Place one cooled puff pastry sheet half on a large cutting board. Invert the

    other baked puff pastry half (so the flatter side is facing up). Use a serrated

    knife to cut the inverted half into 8 rectangles, roughly 1 ½ by 4 ½ inches.

  8. Leave the cut rectangles in the same arrangement and set aside. Pre-cutting them

    now will make it easier to portion your tompouce later.

  9. Vigorously whisk the cooled custard to loosen it up, then pipe or spread it over

    the intact baked puff pastry half and smooth out the top.

  10. To make the glaze for your tompouce, whisk together the confectioners' sugar,

    water, and food coloring. Then pour the glaze over the top of the assembled

    pastry and spread it out in an even layer using an offset spatula or the

    back of a spoon.

  11. Using the pre-cut top layer of pastry as a guide, use a sharp knife to cut

    through the layers to slice the entire pastry into pieces.

  12. Carefully wipe off the blade between cuts for super clean slices. Allow the

    glaze set a bit before serving or serve immediately.
